Research Overview

My research is centered on fracture mechanics and structural integrity, with particular emphasis on Linear Elastic Fracture Mechanics (LEFM) and finite element–based numerical investigations. The work focuses on understanding crack-tip mechanics, stress field evolution, and failure behavior under complex loading conditions.

A key aspect of my research approach is the integration of theoretical concepts, numerical simulation, and experimental stress analysis to develop physically meaningful and reliable interpretations of fracture behavior.

Core Research Themes

  • Linear Elastic Fracture Mechanics (LEFM)
  • Stress intensity factor (SIF) evaluation
  • Mixed-mode crack behavior
  • Finite element modeling of cracked components
  • Structural integrity assessment
  • Experimental stress analysis using photoelasticity

Methodological Approach

Numerical investigations are primarily carried out using the finite element method, with careful attention to mesh design, crack-tip singularity representation, and convergence behavior. Parametric modeling and automation are implemented using ANSYS APDL and ABAQUS with Python scripting.

Experimental stress analysis, particularly photoelasticity with circular polariscope setups, is used as a qualitative and interpretative tool to visualize stress fields and validate trends observed in numerical simulations.

Research Collaboration & Future Directions
