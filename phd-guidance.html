Choosing a Research Problem

A good PhD topic should balance theoretical depth, computational feasibility, and practical relevance. In fracture mechanics and computational solid mechanics, clarity in problem formulation is more important than problem complexity.

Common Mistakes in FEM-Based Research

  • Insufficient mesh refinement near singularities
  • Lack of convergence studies
  • Improper boundary condition representation
  • Over-reliance on software without physical interpretation
  • No experimental or analytical validation

Validation & Interpretation

Numerical results must be interpreted within the framework of fracture mechanics theory. Where possible, experimental stress analysis techniques such as photoelasticity can provide valuable qualitative insight into stress distribution trends.

Academic Writing & Publication

A strong research paper clearly states the problem, methodology, validation strategy, and limitations. Reviewers typically look for consistency between theory, numerical modeling, and interpretation of results.
