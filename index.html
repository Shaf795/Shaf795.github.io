Welcome

This website is developed as a comprehensive academic and learning platform for Mechanical Engineering students, researchers, and professionals. It focuses on developing strong fundamentals in solid mechanics while bridging theory with numerical simulation and real-world structural integrity problems.

The content here reflects my academic training and teaching philosophy, with a particular emphasis on fracture mechanics, stress analysis, and finite element modeling using industry-standard tools.

Core Focus Areas

Stress Analysis

Fundamentals of stress, strain, elasticity, and failure theories explained with physical interpretation and engineering relevance.

Finite Element Method (FEM)

Conceptual understanding of FEM, element behavior, meshing strategies, convergence, and best practices for accurate simulations.

Fracture Mechanics (LEFM)

Linear Elastic Fracture Mechanics, stress intensity factors, mixed-mode loading, crack modeling, and benchmark problems.

Experimental Stress Analysis (Photoelasticity)

Photoelasticity as an experimental technique for visualizing stress distribution and stress concentration using polarized light, with emphasis on circular polariscope setup and interpretation of fringe patterns.

Who This Website Is For

Undergraduate & Postgraduate Students

Clear explanations, structured learning paths, and simulation-oriented understanding of core mechanical engineering subjects.

PhD Scholars & Researchers

Guidance on fracture mechanics research, FEM-based investigations, modeling strategies, and interpretation of results.

Industry & Recruiters

A clear representation of technical expertise in computational mechanics, structural integrity, and engineering problem-solving.

Simulation Tools & Methods

The platform includes structured tutorials and insights into commonly used computational tools for mechanical engineers and researchers.

  • ANSYS APDL – Parametric modeling and fracture simulations
  • ABAQUS with Python – Automation, scripting, and post-processing
  • MATLAB – Numerical methods, validation, and visualization
